Care Guide

These jackets are built for bad weather and real life. A little care will keep the colours loud and the fabric performing through many rainy seasons.

Washing

  • Turn your jacket inside out before washing.
  • Machine wash cold on a gentle cycle.
  • Use a mild liquid detergent.
  • Avoid fabric softeners, bleach, and harsh stain removers. They can damage the waterproof coating and fade the print.
  • Wash with similar colours to prevent any colour transfer.
  • Close all zippers and snaps before washing to protect the hardware.

Drying

  • Do not tumble dry. Heat wears down waterproof layers over time.
  • Hang to dry in the shade instead of direct sunlight.
  • Hang on a sturdy hanger to maintain the jacket's shape.
  • Make sure the jacket is completely dry before storing it.
  • If you're in a rush, hang it in a well-ventilated space with good air flow.

Ironing and Heat

  • If you need to smooth out wrinkles, use a cool iron on the reverse side only.
  • Never iron directly on the printed side—this will damage the artwork.
  • Do not use a steamer on the print side.
  • Do not dry clean. The chemicals can break down waterproof coatings.

After Heavy Rain or Mud

  • Shake off excess water after wearing.
  • Rinse off visible mud or dirt with cool water before washing.
  • Hang in a ventilated space and let it dry fully before packing it away.
  • Don't leave it crumpled in a bag—this can cause mildew and damage the fabric.

Storage

  • Store your jacket on a hanger in a cool, dry place.
  • Avoid storing in direct sunlight for extended periods—UV can fade colours.
  • Don't store your jacket while it's damp. Always make sure it's fully dry.
  • If storing for off-season, keep it in a breathable garment bag (not plastic).

Repairs and Warranty

  • Rips happen. If your jacket takes a hit, email us at support@hogansalley.com with a photo.
  • We'll help with repair options or offer a discounted replacement if it's beyond fixing.
  • All jackets come with a 1-year warranty against manufacturing defects.
  • Normal wear and tear (fading from UV, zipper wear from heavy use) isn't covered, but we're here to help extend your jacket's life.

Maintaining Waterproofing

Over time, the DWR (durable water repellent) coating on your jacket will wear down. This is normal. You'll notice water soaking in instead of beading up.

How to restore it:

Wash your jacket following the instructions above. While it's still damp, tumble dry on low heat for 20 minutes, or use a cool iron over the fabric (not the print). The heat reactivates the DWR coating.

If that doesn't work, you can reapply a spray-on or wash-in DWR treatment. Follow the product instructions carefully and avoid getting it on the printed areas.

Look after your jacket and it will keep looking after you on every grey, wet, sideways-rain kind of day.

Built to last, not to landfill

We're not a fast-fashion brand. These jackets are designed to survive years of daily wear, not a single season.

We use durable fabrics, quality hardware, and construction methods that prioritize longevity. The best thing you can do for the planet is buy less and keep your gear longer.

When your jacket eventually reaches the end of its life, email us. We're working on a take-back program to recycle old jackets into new products.
