The Sanitation Collection

Clean Sweep

Before anyone else is awake, someone is sweeping the streets. This print honors the invisible labor that keeps cities running — the poetry of urban maintenance rendered in brooms and swirling color.

The Story

The Inspiration

The Infrastructure of Care

Every city has its rhythms. The early morning street sweeper. The truck that comes while you sleep. The workers who ensure that by the time you step outside, the world feels fresh again.

The Pattern

Brooms & Flow

Grey-sketched brooms emerge from swirling blues, reds, and greens — maintenance tools transformed into abstract art. The flowing white lines trace the paths these workers take, the choreography of cleanliness we rarely see.

From the Barbican's brutalist concrete to Olympic Village's green architecture — this jacket finds beauty in systems that work.

Urban Poetry

Where Systems Flow

London's early morning cobblestones. Seattle's industrial SoDo. Vancouver's Olympic Village seawall. The Sanitation print honors the invisible infrastructure that makes urban life possible.

Grey-sketched brooms amid swirling organic shapes — infrastructure as art, maintenance as meditation.
