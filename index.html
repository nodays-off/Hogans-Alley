WHY WE EXIST

Why is everyone dressed like the sky?

Most rain jackets are designed to disappear. Black, navy, grey, maybe one safe yellow. That works for hiking gear.

City rain is different. We ride buses, skate in drizzle, walk dogs in sideways rain, stand under leaky bus shelters – and we still want to feel like ourselves.

Hogan's Alley started as a protest against boring rainwear. Jackets that actually keep you dry, covered in patterns and colour that look like our streets, our music, and the cultures that built our cities.

  • Built for real rain – Waterproof shells, taped seams, hardware that doesn't quit.
  • Made to be seen – High-contrast artwork, not just logos slapped on a blank shell.
  • For grey cities – Designed for places where the sky forgets how to be blue.

Born from long, wet winters.

Hogan's Alley started in a city where it can rain for weeks without the sky changing. On the street, everyone blended into black and grey shells. At home, our walls and sketchbooks were full of colour, symbols, and stories.

We decided to put that energy on the outside. Each jacket print starts as an original painting. Every drop you see on the fabric was once a brushstroke.
