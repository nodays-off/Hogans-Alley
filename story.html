The story behind the colour.

Hogan's Alley didn't start in a design studio. It started on grey streets, in sketchbooks and spare bedrooms, with paint and long rainy days.

Long winters, bright ideas.

We grew up in cities where rain isn't an event, it's a season. Months of low clouds, puddles at the bus stop, and umbrellas turned inside out.

On the street, everyone wore the same black and grey shells. At home, the walls were full of colour and pattern. On our tables, notebooks filled with faces, symbols, and stories were piling up.

At some point the question became obvious: why are we hiding the most interesting part of us under the dullest jackets we own?

So we started painting. And then we started figuring out how to turn those paintings into jackets that could actually survive a Vancouver November.

From painting to pattern.

Every Hogan's Alley print starts as a hand-painted piece. Acrylic on canvas or board. No stock patterns, no clip art.

We layer shapes and symbols that come from our own experiences, cultural references, and the energy of the cities we live in. Then we work with pattern makers to translate that artwork into an all-over print that can live on fabric.

The patterns have stories

Libation celebrates shared drinks and late-night gatherings. Market Circuit pulls from coin imagery and the energy of night markets. Stormwave is literally inspired by watching a real storm roll through the city.

We don't make patterns that look cool just for the sake of it. Every print comes from somewhere real.

Made for rainy-season people.

Our jackets are for anyone who lives under grey skies but refuses to blend in.

Students sprinting for class in Vancouver. DJs rolling gear through drizzle in Seattle. Kids jumping into every puddle in London. Artists heading to studio openings when the weather turns. Skaters who don't stop just because it's wet.

If the weather app is always threatening rain where you live, you're our people.

And if you're tired of looking like the sky, we made these for you.

How we build them

These aren't fashion rain jackets. They're built to actually work in real weather.

10K Waterproof Shell

We use breathable, waterproof fabrics rated for sustained rain. Taped seams keep water out at every stitch.

Tested in Real Conditions

We test fits over hoodies. We yank hoods in wind. We check pockets with cold fingers. If it doesn't work in a Vancouver October, it doesn't ship.

Built to Last

Quality hardware, reinforced stress points, and construction that's meant to survive years of daily wear. These aren't single-season pieces.

Then We Add the Art

Once we know the jacket works, we layer on the prints. Full-coverage artwork that turns functional gear into something you actually want to wear.

About the name

Hogan's Alley was a historic Black neighborhood in Vancouver that was demolished in the 1960s to make way for a viaduct. It was a place of culture, community, and resistance—a space where people created their own energy despite everything around them.

We chose the name because it represents what this brand is about: taking space, creating colour, and refusing to disappear into the background.

A portion of our proceeds goes to organizations working to preserve and celebrate Black history in Vancouver and other rainy cities we serve.
