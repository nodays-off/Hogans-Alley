The Name

Hogan's Alley

Our name honors Vancouver's historic Black neighbourhood — a vibrant community that thrived from the 1930s to the late 1960s in the blocks around Park Lane and Union Street. It was the heart of Black Vancouver: a place of music, culture, entrepreneurship, and resilience.

Jazz clubs, chicken joints, barbershops, and rooming houses lined the streets. It was where Jimi Hendrix's grandmother, Nora Hendrix, ran a rooming house. Where the Harlem Nocturne club hosted legends. Where a community built something beautiful against the odds.

"In 1967, the City of Vancouver demolished Hogan's Alley to build the Georgia Viaduct. An entire community was displaced, scattered, erased from the map — but not from memory."

By adopting this name, we bring light to that history. We carry forward the creative spirit of those musicians, artists, and entrepreneurs who called this neighbourhood home.

Sustainability

Plastic Bottles, Reimagined

Every Hogan's Alley jacket is made from recycled PET plastic — the same material in water bottles and food containers. Instead of ending up in landfills, oceans, and waterways, that plastic becomes the fabric you wear.

The Artist

Joel "Chidi" Sydenham

Visual Artist & Creative Partner — London, UK

Chidi is a British-Nigerian visual artist and poet whose work explores the connections between all living things. His art is deeply influenced by his cultural experiences and his belief in an underlying unity between all things in existence.

"The intention with everything I practice is to uplift people, build social bridges, and remind everyone that there is endless beauty within them."

We work closely with Chidi to develop the themes behind each print collection. His vision aligns perfectly with ours: celebrating interconnectedness, honoring cultural heritage, and creating work that uplifts. Our repeating patterns pay homage to traditional African wax prints while bringing them into a contemporary Canadian context.

The Maker

Gwen Lutz & Symmetry Patterns

Full-Service Pattern Studio — Vancouver, BC

Every Hogan's Alley jacket is expertly crafted by Gwen at Symmetry Patterns, a Vancouver-based pattern studio that has been our partner from day one. Her attention to detail and commitment to quality matches our own.

Symmetry Patterns isn't just a manufacturer — they're a true collaborator. Gwen has helped us refine our designs, test durability, and bring our vision to life with expert craftsmanship. Working with a local small business means we can maintain the highest quality standards while supporting our community.

The Founders

Three Friends, One Vision

We're three millennials who immigrated to Canada in the mid-2000s and became fast friends. Together with our artist Chidi, we draw on inspiration from our experiences back home to create prints that celebrate African heritage in a Canadian context — paying homage to traditional wax fabrics through bold, contemporary patterns.
